What's a timestamp?

Google search data time stamps are in the unix-microsecond format by default. In this format time is stored as the number of microseconds elapsed since the moment Dennis Ritchie (creator of Unix and the C language, mother of dragons etc.) uttered the words "let there be light", that is 1-1-1970. Excel however expects timestamps in the format of the number of days begining with the wholly unremarkable date of 1-1-1900.

To convert the timestamps to the excel format:

  1. Divide the timestamps with the number of microseconds in a day, that is 86400*1000000
  2. Add the number of days between 1-1-1900 and 1-1-1970, that is 25569
  3. The timestamps are all set to GMT, to convert to IST, we must add 5 hours and 30 minutes to the timestamps or 5.5/24 days.

Putting these together, in excel the formula is =$A2/86400000000+25569+(5.5/24)
